Sifan Liu – Home page

 

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Statistical Science at Duke University. My research focuses on statistical and computational methods for rigorous and efficient inference, with a focus on the adaptive nature of modeling and analysis in modern data science. I am particularly interested in sampling algorithms with applications to Bayesian inference, selective inference, and generative modeling.

Before joining Duke, I was a Research Scientist at the Center for Computational Mathematics at the Flatiron Institute (2024–2025). I received my Ph.D. in Statistics from Stanford University in 2024 and my B.S. in Mathematics from Tsinghua University in 2019. Here is my CV.

If you are interested in my research, feel free to get in touch!
